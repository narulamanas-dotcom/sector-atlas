Network Infrastructure & Connectivity

Towers, telecom carriers, cable operators, and satellite providers that form the physical and wireless backbone of communications. These are the pipes and rails — businesses with regulatory moats, long-term contracts, and capital-intensive infrastructure that creates natural barriers to entry. They sit beneath the content and platform layers because nothing digital works without connectivity.

Content & IP Owners

Studios, music labels, sports leagues, and gaming publishers that create and own the intellectual property flowing through infrastructure to reach audiences. Their profitability depends on content spend discipline, franchise value, and the secular shift from linear to digital distribution. Live events and music are structurally advantaged as formats streaming cannot commoditize.

Platforms & Monetization

Digital advertising platforms, streaming services, social networks, and publishers that aggregate audiences and monetize attention. Revenue is driven by ad cycles, user engagement, and ARPU expansion. The Google-Meta duopoly captures 50%+ of digital ad spend. The secular shift from analog to digital advertising is approximately 70% complete globally.

Ad Cycle

Corporate marketing budgets expand and contract with economic confidence. Digital captures an increasing share of total ad spend (~70%+). Google and Meta dominate; ad tech infrastructure (TTD, DV, IAS) benefits from higher programmatic volume during expansions.

Streaming Shift

The secular migration from linear TV to streaming is reshaping content distribution, advertising, and consumer spending. Cord-cutting 2.0 now includes broadband losses for cable operators as fixed wireless gains share. Ad-tier adoption extends the streaming revenue model.

Regulatory & Antitrust

Google faces dual antitrust actions (search remedies + ad tech monopoly ruling). Live Nation/Ticketmaster faces DOJ breakup suit. Net neutrality, spectrum policy, and sports betting regulation all shape competitive dynamics across the sector.

AI Disruption

AI search threatens publisher traffic and ad-supported business models. AI-generated content could commoditize music and video IP. Google and Meta are investing $300B+ combined in AI capex. AI ad creative tools are transforming campaign optimization and advertiser ROAS.
