Sourcing, Processing & Ingredients

Agricultural commodity processors, protein packers, and specialty ingredient suppliers that form the upstream input layer. Margins are driven by crush spreads, origination volumes, and protein cycle economics rather than brand premiums. Capital-intensive, cyclical, and exposed to weather, geopolitics, and commodity prices. The specialty ingredient shift is creating a bifurcation between commodity toll processors and higher-margin specialty suppliers.

Brand Owners & Manufacturers

The core value-creation layer — converting commodity inputs into branded products with pricing power through habit formation, shelf-space dominance, and marketing spend. The brand-to-commodity spread is the fundamental metric. Spans packaged foods, beverages, tobacco, household products, and personal care. Winners are global snacking platforms, tollbridge distributors (KO, PG), and niche insurgents riding health trends.

Distribution, Wholesale & Retail

Downstream gatekeepers controlling consumer access to all upstream products. WMT controls ~26% of US grocery spend with unmatched negotiating leverage. COST's membership model generates $4.8B+ in recurring fees. Retailers are transitioning from product margin businesses to platform businesses — advertising, data monetization, and marketplace revenue are the new high-margin profit pools.

Consumer Wallet

Real wage growth vs. food CPI determines trade-down/trade-up behavior. When food inflation outpaces wages, private label gains share and dollar stores benefit. When wages lead, premiumization resumes and brand owners recover volume.

GLP-1 & Health

GLP-1 drugs (Ozempic, Mounjaro) could structurally reduce caloric intake, pressuring packaged food volumes. Conversely, protein-forward and functional nutrition categories may benefit. The adoption curve and food consumption impact data are the key unknowns.

Input Cost Cycle

Cocoa at 3-4x historical prices pressures chocolate margins. Grain, pulp, protein, and resin costs cycle through the value chain with 6-12 month lags. Brand owners with pricing power pass through costs; private label narrows the gap when inputs spike.

Retailer Power

WMT and COST wield growing leverage over CPG suppliers. Private label penetration at record highs (~25% in food). Retail media networks (WMT Connect, Kroger Precision Marketing) are extracting incremental margin from brand owners for shelf placement and data access.
