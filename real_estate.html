Upstream: Exploration & Production

Integrated majors, independent E&Ps, natural gas producers, royalty companies, and uranium miners that find and extract hydrocarbons (and nuclear fuel) from subsurface reservoirs. The 2024 consolidation wave cemented scale advantages among fewer, larger operators. Post-2020 capital discipline means managing for FCF per share rather than production growth. Inventory depth and breakeven quality are the key differentiators.

Midstream: Infrastructure & Transportation

Pipeline operators, gas processors, NGL fractionators, and LNG export terminals that move molecules from supply basins to demand centers. Fee-based and take-or-pay contracts create utility-like cash flows with energy sector growth exposure. The structural moat is permitting — new interstate pipelines take 5-10 years to build, creating scarcity value for existing systems.

Downstream: Refining & Services

Refiners converting crude into gasoline, diesel, and jet fuel, plus oilfield service companies providing drilling, completion, and subsea technology. US refiners benefit from structural advantages: no new capacity since 1977 and access to discounted WTI crude. OFS companies provide the equipment and technology for exploration — offshore drillers are in the strongest upcycle since 2014.

Oil Price Regime

Brent/WTI level and OPEC+ discipline vs. US shale supply response. OPEC+ spare capacity (~5-6M bpd) acts as a ceiling on oil prices. The single most important global oil supply variable determining E&P profitability and capital allocation.

Capital Discipline

Post-2020 E&P revolution: limiting reinvestment to 50-60% of cash flow and returning the remainder via dividends and buybacks. The single most important structural change in energy — companies now manage for FCF per share rather than production growth.

Energy Transition

LNG as bridge fuel, CCS/CCUS development, hydrogen timeline, and electrification pace. Nuclear renaissance driven by AI data center power demand. The transition creates both risk (demand displacement) and opportunity (new infrastructure investment).

Geopolitical Supply Risk

OPEC+ cohesion, Russia sanctions enforcement, Middle East disruption risk, and Venezuela/Iran supply potential. Geopolitical risk premiums in oil prices directly impact E&P cash flows and midstream volume growth trajectories.
