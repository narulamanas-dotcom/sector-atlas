Equity REITs (Asset Owners)

12 sub-industries from industrial/logistics to diversified, spanning physical assets across every major property type. The lease structure — duration, escalator mechanism, and tenant credit quality — determines the risk/return profile. Ranges from nightly repricing (hotels) to 99-year terms (ground leases).

Development & Operating Companies

Master-planned community developers, lot developers, and asset managers that create value through land entitlement, construction, and operating oversight. Higher risk/return profile than stabilized REITs — valued on NAV and land bank optionality rather than FFO.

Services, Brokerage & RE Tech

CRE services firms providing brokerage, advisory, property management, and investment management (CBRE, JLL), plus PropTech platforms digitizing real estate transactions, data, and search (CoStar, Zillow). Asset-light business models with significant recurring revenue components.

Interest Rates

Federal funds rate, 10-year Treasury yield, and the transmission to CRE cap rates and REIT valuations. The single most important macro variable. Rate changes propagate through the sector with a 6–12 month lag via public-to-private arbitrage.

Demographics

Baby boomer aging into senior housing (80+ cohort acceleration), millennial household formation driving rental demand, and population migration patterns (Sunbelt vs. gateway). The most predictable demand drivers in real estate.

Technology Disruption

E-commerce vs. brick-and-mortar retail, WFH vs. office, AI data center demand, and PropTech digitization of transactions. Technology is simultaneously destroying demand for some property types and creating explosive demand for others.

Housing Policy & Regulation

Rent control legislation, zoning reform, 1031 exchange political risk, REIT tax structure preservation, and building code requirements. Regulatory risk varies dramatically by geography and property type.
