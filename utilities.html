Regulated Infrastructure Owners

Vertically integrated electric utilities, T&D pure plays, multi-utilities, gas LDCs, and water utilities — all earning allowed returns on rate base set by state PUCs and FERC. The energy transition, data center load growth, and grid modernization are driving the largest CapEx super-cycle in decades. Sub-industry differentiation spans electric generation through water treatment, but the unifying economics are rate base × allowed ROE.

Contracted & Renewable Generators

Large-scale renewable energy platforms and yieldcos with revenue secured via long-term PPAs rather than rate cases. Brookfield Renewable is the global benchmark; AES is the most aggressive transition story. YieldCos (CWEN, XIFR) hold operating assets and distribute CAFD. The model bridges regulated and merchant worlds — contracted capacity provides quasi-regulated returns without the regulatory apparatus.

Merchant Power & Competitive Supply

Independent power producers selling into wholesale markets (PJM, ERCOT, CAISO). Transformed from distressed cyclicals into AI/data center beneficiaries. Nuclear fleet scarcity value is the defining narrative — existing nuclear plants are irreplaceable carbon-free baseload. CEG+Calpine merger creates the largest US power producer. Data center PPAs are converting merchant earnings to quasi-contracted streams.

Interest Rates & Cost of Capital

Utilities are capital-intensive, leveraged businesses. The 10-yr Treasury is the single most important macro variable — above 5% it compresses P/E multiples by 2-3x. YieldCos and leveraged renewables are most rate-sensitive. FFO/Debt thresholds for credit ratings create hard funding constraints.

Regulatory Constructiveness

The earned-vs-allowed ROE spread is the ultimate quality signal. Constructive states (WI, IN, MN) allow timely cost recovery and infrastructure trackers. Adversarial states (CA, NY) create regulatory lag. Each state commission has its own philosophy — jurisdiction is destiny for regulated utilities.

Power Demand Growth

AI/data center buildout is creating the first structural electricity demand inflection in 20 years. Hyperscaler commitments imply 50+ GW of incremental demand by 2028. Utilities with territory exposure (VA, GA, TX, AZ, ID) earn asymmetric rate base growth. Nuclear scarcity premium benefits merchant generators.

Clean Energy Policy (IRA)

IRA provides PTC/ITC for wind, solar, storage, and nuclear through 2030-2032. OBBB shortened some credits. Projects must begin construction by July 2026. Accelerates near-term renewable deployment but creates potential post-2030 CapEx cliff. Nuclear and storage retain longer credit windows.
