Tier 1 — Product Creators & Brand Owners

Design, engineer, manufacture, and brand. Auto OEMs, parts suppliers, apparel/luxury brands, homebuilders, and leisure product makers. Pricing power comes from brand equity, IP, and manufacturing scale. Most capital-intensive tier with highest input cost sensitivity.

Tier 2 — Experience & Service Operators

Hotels, casinos, restaurants, and education services. Asset-light franchise models (MCD, MAR, HLT) are the highest-quality businesses. Experience economy beneficiary tier. Tollbridge franchisors earn royalties on system sales with minimal capex.

Tier 3 — Distributors & Retailers

Last mile to consumer: e-commerce platforms, home improvement, specialty retail, auto parts, and distributors. Off-price (TJX, ROST, BURL) wins in all environments. Auto parts (AZO, ORLY) benefit from aging vehicle fleet. Amazon dominates broadline e-commerce.

Consumer Confidence

Conference Board and Michigan sentiment indices drive discretionary spending willingness. When confidence drops below 80, high-discretionary categories contract first while value retailers gain share.

Interest Rates / Housing

Fed funds rate, mortgage rates, and housing turnover cascade through homebuilders, home improvement, household durables, and auto financing. Rate cuts unlock the lock-in effect and stimulate big-ticket spending.

Employment & Wages

Non-farm payrolls, unemployment rate, and real wage growth determine the consumer's ability to spend. Restaurant traffic, travel bookings, and retail comps all correlate with employment strength.
